# Monitor risks

> Identifying, examining, and recognizing/justifying any improbability in investment decision making.

## Overview

The monitor risks process is a critical component of the Enterprise Risk & Compliance function within an organization. It encompasses the systematic approach to monitor risks, ensuring that all activities are performed consistently, efficiently, and in alignment with organizational objectives. This process establishes the framework through which monitor risks is executed, monitored, and continuously improved to deliver value across the enterprise.

Within the APQC Process Classification Framework (hierarchy 11.1.3.5), this activity supports the broader "Manage Enterprise Risk, Compliance, Remediation, Resiliency" category. Effective execution requires cross-functional collaboration, clear accountability, and robust governance mechanisms. Organizations that mature this process typically see improved operational performance, reduced risk exposure, and stronger alignment between tactical activities and strategic goals.

## Industry Variations

### Financial Services

Extensive regulatory frameworks (Basel III, Dodd-Frank), stress testing requirements, and real-time risk monitoring across trading and credit portfolios.

### Healthcare

HIPAA compliance, patient safety risk management, clinical trial oversight, and medical device regulatory tracking.

### Energy & Utilities

Environmental compliance (EPA, NERC), operational safety risk, pipeline integrity management, and disaster resilience planning.
