# Share knowledge of specific risks across other parts of the organization

> Sharing information about risks and resilience strategies of business operations across the organization so that prospective risks can be avoided.

## Overview

The share knowledge of specific risks across other parts of the organization process is a critical component of the Enterprise Risk & Compliance function within an organization. It encompasses the systematic approach to share knowledge of specific risks across other parts of the organization, ensuring that all activities are performed consistently, efficiently, and in alignment with organizational objectives. This process establishes the framework through which share knowledge of specific risks across other parts of the organization is executed, monitored, and continuously improved to deliver value across the enterprise.

Within the APQC Process Classification Framework (hierarchy 11.4.5), this process supports the broader "Manage Enterprise Risk, Compliance, Remediation, Resiliency" category. Effective execution requires cross-functional collaboration, clear accountability, and robust governance mechanisms. Organizations that mature this process typically see improved operational performance, reduced risk exposure, and stronger alignment between tactical activities and strategic goals.
