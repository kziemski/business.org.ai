# Analyze customer revenue trend

## Overview

Analyzing the revenue stream generated by the sale of the organization's products/services in order to identify trends therein. Examine data relating to the inflow of revenue from individual/groups of customers in order to identify patterns in the generation and sustenance of receivables. Conduct statistical analysis over the stream of revenue collected and the point of origin associated with each unit of sale through metrics such as the accounting rate of return, the GAAP revenue over a given period, and customer lifetime revenue.

## Industry Variations

Financial management in petroleum downstream addresses unique aspects of the refining and fuel distribution business including volatile commodity prices, complex transfer pricing for intercompany crude and product movements, and significant capital investment requirements. Margin management focuses on crack spreads between crude costs and refined product prices. Tax accounting must handle fuel excise taxes, environmental fees, and renewable fuel credit trading. Working capital management balances crude oil inventory carrying costs with supply security requirements.
