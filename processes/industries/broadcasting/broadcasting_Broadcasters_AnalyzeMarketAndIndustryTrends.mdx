# Analyze market and industry trends

## Overview

Examining large-scale shifts and trends, with relevance to the organization's products/services. Vet the industrial and larger market landscape to identify broad-based movements that could have a direct or tangential impact on the uptake of the organization's products/services. Examine, among other things, the market capitalization of similar products, the profitability of organizations offering competing products/services, the stock price of key vendors/suppliers in the organizational value-chain, the rate and scale of innovation activity in the organization's product/service category, the price and availability of raw materials, and the shelf-life of similar products/services. Conduct primary and secondary research, and consider enlisting professional services.

## Industry Variations

Market and industry trend analysis in broadcasting requires specialized focus areas:

- **Cord-cutting dynamics**: Tracking the shift from traditional cable/satellite subscriptions to streaming platforms and its impact on advertising revenue and distribution models
- **Ratings ecosystem**: Nielsen ratings, streaming metrics, and social media engagement create a complex measurement landscape unique to broadcasting
- **Content licensing trends**: Analyzing the competitive landscape for sports rights, syndication deals, and original programming acquisitions
- **Technology adoption**: Monitoring ATSC 3.0 (NextGen TV) adoption, streaming technology evolution, and connected TV penetration
- **Advertising market shifts**: Understanding programmatic advertising, addressable TV advertising, and the convergence of digital and traditional ad buying
- **Regulatory landscape**: FCC policy changes, spectrum auctions, and media ownership rules significantly impact market dynamics
- **Demographic viewing patterns**: Generational differences in content consumption habits between linear TV, streaming, and social video platforms
