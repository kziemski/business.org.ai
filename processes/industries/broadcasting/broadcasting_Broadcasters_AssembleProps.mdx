## Industry Variations

Props management in broadcasting differs from film production in several key ways:

- **Inventory management**: Studio productions maintain extensive prop inventories for recurring shows versus single-production prop acquisition in film
- **Product placement coordination**: Props often involve advertiser relationships, requiring brand approval, placement tracking, and integration billing
- **Daily production turnover**: News, talk shows, and daily programs require rapid prop sourcing and staging compared to planned film productions
- **Food styling requirements**: Cooking shows and food segments require specialized prop handling for both appearance and safety
- **Rights clearance**: Props featuring logos, artwork, or branded items require trademark clearance for broadcast
- **Period accuracy**: Historical dramas and period pieces require research-verified props that meet broadcast standards
- **Safety regulations**: Props must meet both union safety requirements and broadcast standards (no real weapons, safe food handling, etc.)
