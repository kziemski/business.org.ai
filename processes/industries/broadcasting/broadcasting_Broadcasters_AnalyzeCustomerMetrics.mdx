# Analyze customer metrics

## Overview

Studying all measures of the customer's behavior and conduct toward the organization's offerings in order to glean insight and identify patterns into their decision making. Closely examine all categories of data sets over a customer base. Analyze data points related to customer loyalty, retention, value, conversion, level of satisfaction, attrition, etc. Flesh out measures for an all-encompassing analysis that provides a macro-level picture of the customer's behavior and mindset related to the organization's products/services.

## Industry Variations

Customer metrics in broadcasting differ significantly from traditional retail or service industries:

- **Audience measurement complexity**: Nielsen ratings, streaming metrics, and social engagement represent different "customers" (viewers vs. advertisers) with distinct success metrics
- **Dual revenue model**: Customer metrics must track both viewer engagement (ratings, time spent, completion rates) and advertiser satisfaction (CPM delivery, campaign performance)
- **Subscriber vs. advertising metrics**: Cable networks track subscriber counts and carriage fees while also monitoring ad-supported metrics
- **Content performance attribution**: Understanding which programs drive tune-in, subscriber acquisition, and cross-platform engagement
- **Cord-cutting and churn analysis**: Tracking the migration of viewers from traditional distribution to streaming platforms
- **Demographic value variations**: Same audience size can have dramatically different advertiser value based on age, income, and purchasing behavior demographics
- **Multi-platform journey tracking**: Measuring customer value across linear TV, streaming apps, website, social media, and podcast engagement
