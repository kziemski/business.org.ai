## Industry Variations

Restriction and embargo management in broadcasting involves complex temporal and territorial controls:

- **Sports blackout rules**: NFL, MLB, and other leagues mandate local blackout periods for non-sellout games requiring dynamic restriction application
- **Content holdback windows**: Theatrical-to-streaming release windows, pay TV exclusivity periods, and international distribution timing requirements
- **News embargo compliance**: Breaking news stories from pool coverage or wire services may have release time restrictions
- **Talent consent restrictions**: Some content may have usage restrictions based on talent contract terms or likeness rights
- **Advertiser conflict restrictions**: Content may be restricted from airing adjacent to competitor advertisements
- **Time-period restrictions**: Certain content may only air during specific dayparts due to content ratings or FCC indecency rules
- **Platform-specific restrictions**: Content may be cleared for linear broadcast but restricted from streaming, DVR, or on-demand platforms
