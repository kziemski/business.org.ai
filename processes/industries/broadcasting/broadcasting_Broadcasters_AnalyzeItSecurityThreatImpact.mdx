## Industry Variations

IT security threat analysis in broadcasting involves unique infrastructure considerations:

- **Critical infrastructure status**: Broadcast facilities are often designated as critical infrastructure with special DHS/CISA threat intelligence requirements
- **Live broadcast disruption**: Security threats that could interrupt live broadcasts (news, sports, emergency alerts) carry exceptional reputational and regulatory risk
- **Content piracy prevention**: Protecting pre-release content and live streams from unauthorized redistribution requires specialized security measures
- **Transmission system security**: Protecting broadcast transmission infrastructure from interference or hijacking (especially EAS systems)
- **IP-based workflow vulnerabilities**: Modern IP video production workflows introduce network security risks in previously isolated broadcast systems
- **Ransomware targeting**: Media companies face elevated ransomware risk due to content value and time-sensitive production schedules
- **Third-party access control**: Production partners, freelancers, and remote contributors require secure access to content systems
