## Industry Variations

Compliance assessment in broadcasting involves a complex web of industry-specific regulations:

- **FCC license compliance**: Maintaining compliance with broadcast license conditions, public interest obligations, and ownership rules
- **Content standards**: Monitoring indecency standards, children's programming requirements (Children's Television Act), and political broadcasting rules
- **EAS obligations**: Ensuring Emergency Alert System equipment, testing, and procedures meet FCC and FEMA requirements
- **Accessibility compliance**: Meeting FCC closed captioning, video description, and Section 255 accessibility requirements
- **Political advertising rules**: Maintaining compliance with equal time provisions, lowest unit charge, and advertising disclosure requirements
- **Tower and transmission compliance**: Meeting FAA lighting requirements, RF exposure limits, and interference prevention standards
- **MVPD relationships**: Compliance with retransmission consent, must-carry elections, and syndicated exclusivity rules
