# Analyze demographics

## Overview

Analyzing statistical data relating to the size, distribution, and composition of relevant populations, as well as their characteristics. Perform quantitative analysis over raw data-sets gathered from well-founded sources such as government census or large, private databases. Consider employing primary research in collecting required statistics. Use comprehensive studies (reports, briefs, and articles) to assist with the analysis, in place of raw data.

## Industry Variations

Demographic analysis in broadcasting is fundamental to content strategy and advertising value:

- **Audience composition analysis**: Understanding age, gender, income, education, and household composition of viewers by program and daypart
- **Demo-based pricing**: CPM rates vary dramatically by demographic (18-49, 25-54, Women 25-54) making composition analysis critical
- **Generational viewing patterns**: Analyzing how Boomers, Gen X, Millennials, and Gen Z consume content differently across platforms
- **Geographic reach**: DMA (Designated Market Area) analysis for local stations and regional programming strategies
- **Advertiser category alignment**: Matching audience demographics with advertiser target markets for ad sales and programming
- **Diversity representation**: Analyzing on-screen representation and audience diversity for programming and talent decisions
- **Cord-cutting demographics**: Understanding which demographic segments are shifting to streaming and OTT platforms
