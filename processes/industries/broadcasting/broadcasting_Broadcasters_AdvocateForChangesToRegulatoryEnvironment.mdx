## Industry Variations

Regulatory advocacy in broadcasting addresses a unique set of policy domains:

- **Spectrum policy**: Advocating for favorable treatment in FCC spectrum auctions, repacking, and ATSC 3.0 transition policies
- **Ownership rules**: Lobbying on media ownership caps, cross-ownership restrictions, and merger approval processes
- **Net neutrality and carriage**: Positioning on must-carry, retransmission consent, and cable/streaming distribution requirements
- **Content regulation**: Engaging on indecency rules, children's programming requirements, and political advertising disclosure
- **Copyright and piracy**: Advocating for strong IP protections, DMCA enforcement, and international treaty provisions
- **Localism requirements**: Addressing public interest obligations, local news requirements, and emergency broadcasting mandates
- **Competition policy**: Participating in antitrust reviews of media mergers, streaming platform market power, and sports rights bundling
