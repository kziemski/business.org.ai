## Industry Variations

Content acquisition in broadcasting involves specialized considerations distinct from other media industries:

- **Rights windows and holdbacks**: Content deals specify complex territorial and platform exclusivity windows (theatrical, pay TV, basic cable, streaming, SVOD, AVOD)
- **Sports rights bidding**: Live sports rights involve multi-billion dollar, multi-year packages with exclusive broadcast windows and platform-specific restrictions
- **Upfront marketplace**: Networks acquire scripted content through pilot season and upfront presentations, committing to series orders before production
- **Syndication economics**: Off-network content acquisition involves revenue sharing, advertiser-supported windows, and stripped scheduling requirements
- **International format licensing**: Purchasing rights to adapt international formats (game shows, reality competitions) involves format bibles, consulting fees, and territory restrictions
- **News content sharing**: Affiliate relationships, wire services, and pool coverage arrangements govern news content acquisition and redistribution
- **Library acquisitions**: Catalog deals for classic content require careful rights clearance for music, talent, and underlying intellectual property
