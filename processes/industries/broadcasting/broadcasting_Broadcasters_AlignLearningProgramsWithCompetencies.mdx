# Align learning programs with competencies

## Overview

Aligning the learning programs with the core capabilities and competencies of the organization. Contextualize the training programs so that employees can expand their knowledge base and add new skills in line with the core competencies of the organization.

## Industry Variations

Learning program alignment in broadcasting addresses specialized industry competencies:

- **Technical certification programs**: Broadcast engineers require SBE (Society of Broadcast Engineers) certifications and manufacturer-specific training
- **On-air presentation skills**: Coaching programs for anchors, reporters, and hosts covering delivery, interviewing, and live breaking news handling
- **Editorial standards training**: News organizations maintain rigorous journalism ethics and fact-checking competency requirements
- **Production workflow training**: Multi-camera direction, control room operations, and remote production require specialized instruction
- **Digital and social media skills**: Traditional broadcast professionals need upskilling in digital-first content creation and platform optimization
- **Safety and compliance training**: FCC regulations, EAS procedures, and workplace safety specific to studios and remote operations
- **Emerging technology preparation**: Virtual production, AR/VR, cloud workflows, and AI tools require continuous learning investments
