## Industry Variations

Make-good allocation is a uniquely broadcasting practice with complex business implications:

- **Audience guarantee fulfillment**: When programs underperform their guaranteed ratings, advertisers receive additional ad spots to reach their contracted impression levels
- **Inventory impact analysis**: Make-goods consume available inventory that could otherwise be sold, requiring careful yield management and forecasting
- **Competitive separation**: Make-good spots must maintain advertiser separation requirements (no competitor ads adjacent to each other)
- **Daypart considerations**: Advertisers may prefer make-goods in specific dayparts, requiring negotiation and inventory balancing
- **Cross-platform options**: Modern make-good strategies may include digital impressions, streaming inventory, or social placements as alternatives
- **Seasonality pressures**: High-demand periods like upfront, political seasons, and Q4 make it harder to find quality make-good inventory
- **Stewardship reporting**: Advertisers receive detailed reports on make-good allocation showing how their guaranteed impressions were fulfilled
