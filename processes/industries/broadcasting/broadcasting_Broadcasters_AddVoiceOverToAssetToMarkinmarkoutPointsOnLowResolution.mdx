## Industry Variations

Voice-over and mark-in/mark-out processes in broadcasting have unique technical requirements:

- **Proxy workflow**: Low-resolution proxies enable faster editing review cycles before committing to high-resolution rendering, critical for daily news and sports turnaround
- **Timecode precision**: Frame-accurate marking using SMPTE timecode ensures voice-over synchronization when assets are re-linked to high-resolution masters
- **Multi-language dubbing**: International distribution requires voice-over capability for localization with proper audio mixing and sync
- **Live captioning integration**: Mark points often correlate with caption timing for accessibility compliance (FCC requirements)
- **News package assembly**: Rapid voice-over recording with precise edit points enables same-day news package production
- **Promo and trailer creation**: Marketing departments use low-res mark-in/mark-out for quick promo creation before final conform
- **Remote collaboration**: Cloud-based proxy workflows allow voice talent and editors to collaborate across locations
