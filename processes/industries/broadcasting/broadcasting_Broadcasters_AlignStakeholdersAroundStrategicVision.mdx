# Align stakeholders around strategic vision

## Overview

Orienting those entities, associated with the organization that have a direct bearing on its operations and output, in a way that advances its strategic vision. Map all stakeholders in strategic configurations, within the architectural layout of the marketplace, and position the organization relative to them. (This exercise is undertaken by senior strategy personnel, drawing upon the process Define a business concept and long-term vision [17040].)

## Industry Variations

Stakeholder alignment in broadcasting involves a uniquely complex ecosystem:

- **Affiliate relationships**: Network broadcasters must align local affiliate stations around programming strategies, news sharing arrangements, and brand standards
- **Advertiser partnerships**: Major advertisers often have multi-year commitments that require early visibility into content strategy and audience development plans
- **Talent negotiations**: High-profile on-air talent, show creators, and production companies are key stakeholders whose contracts and creative relationships shape strategic direction
- **Regulatory stakeholders**: FCC commissioners, congressional media committees, and advocacy groups influence spectrum policy, ownership rules, and content regulations
- **Distribution partners**: Cable, satellite, and streaming platform relationships require strategic alignment around carriage agreements and digital rights
- **Union relationships**: Guild and union negotiations (SAG-AFTRA, WGA, DGA, IATSE) fundamentally impact production capacity and labor costs
