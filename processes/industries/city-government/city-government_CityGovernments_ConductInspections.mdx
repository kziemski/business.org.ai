## Industry Variations

City government inspections serve as the primary enforcement mechanism for building, fire, health, and safety codes:

- **Inspection Types**: Cities conduct multiple inspection types including building (foundation, framing, final), fire (annual business inspections), health (food establishments), and housing code (complaint-based)
- **Certification Requirements**: Inspectors typically require ICC certifications in their specialty areas and may need state licensing
- **Mobile Technology**: Field inspectors increasingly use tablets and mobile apps for real-time inspection entry, photo documentation, and permit status updates
- **Reinspection Fees**: Cities charge reinspection fees for failed inspections to recover costs and incentivize contractor compliance
- **Risk-Based Scheduling**: Health and fire inspections often use risk-based frequency, with high-risk occupancies receiving more frequent inspections
