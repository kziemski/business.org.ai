## Industry Variations

Acquiring workspace and facilities in city government involves unique public sector requirements:

- **Public Procurement**: Property acquisitions must follow competitive bidding, council approval, and public hearing requirements depending on transaction value
- **Eminent Domain**: Cities have condemnation authority but must follow due process, just compensation requirements, and public purpose limitations
- **Environmental Review**: NEPA/CEQA environmental reviews may be required for significant facility acquisitions or construction projects
- **Historic Preservation**: Acquisitions may trigger historic review if properties are listed or eligible for historic registers
- **Bond Financing**: Major facility acquisitions often require voter-approved bonds or certificates of participation, adding complexity to the acquisition timeline
