## Industry Variations

City government budget analysis has unique characteristics that distinguish it from private sector budgeting:

- **Fund Accounting**: Cities use governmental fund accounting with separate funds (general fund, capital projects, enterprise funds) rather than single-entity financial statements
- **Legal Appropriations**: Budgets represent legal spending authority; departments cannot exceed appropriated amounts without council approval
- **Revenue Constraints**: Primary revenues come from property taxes, sales taxes, and intergovernmental transfers with limited ability to increase revenue
- **Citizen Participation**: Budget analysis must support public hearings, town halls, and transparency requirements mandated by state law
- **Multi-Year Planning**: Capital improvement programs require 5-10 year budget projections for infrastructure investments
