## Industry Variations

Managing pedestrian and cycling infrastructure reflects growing municipal emphasis on multimodal transportation:

- **Complete Streets Policies**: Many cities have adopted Complete Streets ordinances requiring consideration of all users (pedestrians, cyclists, transit) in roadway projects
- **Active Transportation Plans**: Municipalities develop bicycle and pedestrian master plans identifying network gaps, prioritizing projects, and setting mode share goals
- **Federal Funding**: Projects may qualify for federal TAP (Transportation Alternatives Program) funding requiring compliance with FHWA standards
- **Vision Zero**: Cities adopting Vision Zero commit to eliminating traffic fatalities, often prioritizing protected bike lanes and pedestrian safety improvements
- **Maintenance Equity**: ADA requires cities to prioritize sidewalk repairs in response to complaints and maintain accessible routes throughout the pedestrian network
