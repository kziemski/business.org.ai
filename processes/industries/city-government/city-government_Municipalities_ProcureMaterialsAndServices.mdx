## Industry Variations

Municipal procurement operates under strict public contracting requirements distinct from private sector purchasing:

- **Competitive Bidding**: State law typically requires formal sealed bids for purchases above threshold amounts ($25,000-$100,000 depending on state)
- **Local Preference**: Some cities provide bid preference to local businesses within city limits or regional areas to support economic development
- **MBE/WBE Goals**: Many cities establish goals for minority-owned and women-owned business participation in contracts
- **Cooperative Purchasing**: Cities leverage cooperative purchasing agreements (HGAC, U.S. Communities, NASPO) for volume discounts on common commodities
- **Emergency Procurement**: Emergency purchases may bypass normal competitive requirements but require documentation and council ratification
