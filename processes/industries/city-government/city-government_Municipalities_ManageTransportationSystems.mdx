## Industry Variations

Municipal transportation management encompasses streets, transit, parking, and traffic systems:

- **Multi-Modal Planning**: Cities balance competing needs of vehicles, transit, pedestrians, and cyclists through integrated transportation planning
- **Traffic Signal Systems**: Municipalities operate traffic signal systems, often with adaptive signal control and real-time monitoring through traffic management centers
- **Transit Operations**: Some cities operate municipal transit systems (bus, light rail) directly or through transit authorities requiring coordination on routes, schedules, and capital investments
- **Parking Management**: Cities manage on-street and off-street parking through meters, permits, and municipal parking structures as both a service and revenue source
- **Regional Coordination**: Transportation planning requires coordination with MPOs (Metropolitan Planning Organizations) for federal funding and regional network planning
