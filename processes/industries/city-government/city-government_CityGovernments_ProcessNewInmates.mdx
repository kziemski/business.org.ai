## Industry Variations

New inmate processing in city jails differs from state and federal facilities:

- **Short-Term Detention**: City jails typically hold inmates for 72 hours to one year, focusing on booking and short-term custody rather than long-term incarceration
- **Medical Screening**: Cities must conduct immediate health assessments including mental health, suicide risk, and communicable disease screening under court-established standards
- **Rights Notification**: Processing must ensure timely notification of rights, access to counsel, and bail/bond procedures within constitutionally required timeframes
- **Classification Systems**: Cities use classification systems appropriate for short-term population managing separation requirements for vulnerable populations
- **Documentation Requirements**: Intake documentation must meet state jail standards, court requirements, and potential civil liability considerations
