## Industry Variations

Managing legal and ethical issues in municipal government involves unique public sector considerations:

- **Governmental Immunity**: Cities have limited sovereign immunity but face liability for discretionary vs. ministerial functions and must follow tort claims procedures
- **Ethics Codes**: Municipalities maintain ethics codes governing conflicts of interest, gifts, financial disclosure, and post-employment restrictions for officials and employees
- **Open Government**: Legal compliance includes Brown Act/Sunshine Law requirements for open meetings and public records access
- **Civil Rights Liability**: Section 1983 claims for constitutional violations expose cities to significant liability for police misconduct, due process violations, and discrimination
- **Interlocal Litigation**: Cities may face litigation with other governmental entities over annexation, tax sharing, water rights, or mutual aid disputes
