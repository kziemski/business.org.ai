# Plan and develop cost and quality targets

## Overview

Setting prerequisites for the cost of development and quality standards for the new solutions' portfolio and/or its individual offerings. Set targets for the budget and quality standards for the revamped portfolio of solution offerings. Prepare a plan for the outlay required for revising and adding new product/services. Identify intended levels of quality for these, bearing in mind the existing standards of solutions offered by the organization and its competitors. Enlist senior management executives, particularly those responsible for finance and budgeting, product/service design, manufacturing/processing, delivery, and quality control. Within the Aerospace and Defense industry, this process takes on particular importance due to the sector's emphasis on precision manufacturing, safety-critical systems, and defense program management. Organizations must ensure alignment with FAA, ITAR, DFARS, AS9100 standards while optimizing for efficiency and effectiveness.

## Industry-Specific Considerations

- Compliance with FAA, ITAR, DFARS, AS9100 requirements must be integrated into every step of this process
- Long product lifecycles (20-30 years) directly impacts how this process is designed and executed
- Product and service development must account for precision manufacturing, safety-critical systems, and defense program management requirements
- Aerospace and Defense organizations must balance complex supply chains with security requirements with operational efficiency
