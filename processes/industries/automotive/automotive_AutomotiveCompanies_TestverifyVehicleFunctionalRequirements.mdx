# Test/verify vehicle functional requirements

## Overview

The final processes in developing a new vehicle relates to the verification of the vehicle’s functional requirements. Experts are often consulted to troubleshoot potential problems and to conduct extensive tests, such as track tests and simulated road and driving conditions. The prototype is then reworked, or redesigned, if necessary. The vehicle, supplier durability, and performance are tested, design changes are managed, and vehicles are prepared. Additionally, regulatory certifications are obtained and approval from senior management for final vehicle test and ride are obtained. Once the vehicle is in production, sales volume and customer satisfaction/complaints are verified, quality results are analyzed and countermeasures for market are planned. Within the Automotive industry, this process takes on particular importance due to the sector's emphasis on production efficiency, supply chain coordination, and quality management. Organizations must ensure alignment with NHTSA, EPA, IATF 16949 standards while optimizing for efficiency and effectiveness.
