## Industry Variations

Compensation and incentives in education follow unique patterns compared to corporate settings:

- **Salary Schedules**: K-12 teachers typically follow negotiated step-and-lane salary schedules based on years of experience and educational credentials
- **Performance Pay Debates**: Merit-based compensation remains controversial in education with ongoing debates about appropriate metrics
- **Stipend Structures**: Additional compensation for coaching, club sponsorship, department chair duties, and curriculum development is common
- **Academic Rank Progression**: Higher education uses promotion through faculty ranks (assistant, associate, full professor) with tenure considerations
- **Summer Pay Options**: Teachers may choose between 10-month or 12-month pay distribution for the same annual salary
- **Collective Bargaining**: Compensation changes often require formal negotiation with employee unions and board approval
- **Retention Bonuses**: Hard-to-fill positions (STEM, special education, bilingual) may receive additional incentives
- **Longevity Pay**: Many districts provide additional compensation for long-tenured employees as a retention strategy
