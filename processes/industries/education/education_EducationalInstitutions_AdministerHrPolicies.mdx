## Industry Variations

HR policy administration in education involves sector-specific considerations:

- **Credential Verification**: Teachers must maintain valid state licensure/certification with ongoing professional development requirements
- **Background Check Protocols**: Enhanced background screening requirements for employees working with minors exceed typical employment standards
- **Academic Freedom Policies**: Higher education HR policies must balance institutional oversight with faculty academic freedom protections
- **Tenure Procedures**: Universities have elaborate tenure review processes with specific timelines and appeal mechanisms
- **Union Contracts**: Many HR policies are negotiated through collective bargaining agreements that supersede standard employment policies
- **Student Worker Policies**: Managing graduate assistants and student employees requires policies addressing dual roles and FLSA compliance
- **Title IX Compliance**: Educational HR must include specific policies and training related to sexual harassment and gender equity
- **Mandated Reporter Requirements**: All educational employees typically have mandatory child abuse reporting obligations
