## Industry Variations

Addressing tax inquiries in educational settings involves unique considerations:

- **Tax-Exempt Status Defense**: Institutions must be prepared to document educational activities and public benefit to maintain 501(c)(3) status during IRS audits
- **Unrelated Business Income**: Inquiries often focus on revenue from parking, facilities rentals, and auxiliary services that may trigger UBIT
- **Employment Classification**: Questions frequently arise about proper classification of adjunct faculty, graduate assistants, and student workers
- **International Tax Treaties**: Universities must address inquiries related to foreign national employees and cross-border educational programs
- **Property Tax Appeals**: Educational institutions may need to justify property tax exemptions for facilities used partially for non-educational purposes
- **Scholarship Reporting**: Institutions must accurately report taxable portions of scholarships and fellowships on Form 1098-T
