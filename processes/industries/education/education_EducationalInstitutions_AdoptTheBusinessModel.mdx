## Industry Variations

Adopting business models in education involves considerations distinct from commercial enterprises:

- **Mission-Driven Focus**: Educational business models must prioritize student outcomes and educational access alongside financial sustainability
- **Governance Complexity**: Public schools answer to elected boards, while universities have trustees, faculty senates, and accreditation bodies all influencing model adoption
- **Funding Model Diversity**: Educational models must integrate multiple revenue streams including tuition, government funding, grants, donations, and auxiliary services
- **Stakeholder Approval**: Major model changes may require faculty governance approval, union negotiations, and community input processes
- **Accreditation Constraints**: Business model changes must maintain compliance with regional and program-specific accreditation requirements
- **Public Accountability**: Public institutions have transparency requirements that limit certain commercial business model strategies
- **Long Planning Cycles**: Educational change initiatives typically require multi-year implementation timelines aligned with academic calendars
