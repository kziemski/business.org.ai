## Industry Variations

Assessment administration in education is highly specialized compared to other industries:

- **State Mandated Testing**: K-12 schools must administer state-required standardized assessments with strict security protocols, timing windows, and accommodation requirements
- **Formative vs Summative**: Education uses layered assessment strategies including diagnostic, formative, interim, and summative assessments throughout the learning cycle
- **Accommodations Management**: Students with IEPs or 504 plans require individualized testing accommodations that must be documented and implemented consistently
- **Data Privacy**: Student assessment data is protected under FERPA with strict rules about who can access results and how they can be used
- **Multiple Modalities**: Assessments may be computer-based, paper-based, or performance-based depending on grade level, subject, and student needs
- **Reporting Requirements**: Results must be disaggregated by subgroups and reported to state agencies, parents, and the public within specified timeframes
- **Test Security**: Proctoring, material chain of custody, and testing environment requirements are regulated at the state level
