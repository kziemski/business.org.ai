## Industry Variations

Managing IT licenses in educational institutions requires specialized approaches:

- **Educational Licensing Programs**: Vendors like Microsoft, Adobe, Google, and Apple offer deeply discounted or free educational licenses with specific eligibility requirements
- **FERPA Compliance Requirements**: Software agreements must include data privacy addendums ensuring vendor compliance with student data protection regulations
- **Student License Management**: Tracking licenses for temporary student populations requires automated provisioning and deprovisioning aligned with enrollment
- **COPPA Considerations**: K-12 schools must verify that software used by students under 13 meets Children's Online Privacy Protection Act requirements
- **Consortium Purchasing**: Educational institutions often leverage buying cooperatives to negotiate better licensing terms
- **Open Educational Resources**: Growing emphasis on OER and open-source alternatives to reduce licensing costs
- **1:1 Device Programs**: Managing licenses across student-assigned devices requires mobile device management integration
- **Seasonal Licensing**: Some licenses accommodate academic calendar fluctuations in active user counts
