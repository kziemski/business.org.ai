## Industry Variations

Stakeholder alignment in education involves a uniquely diverse constituency:

- **Elected Governance**: Public school boards are elected officials who may have divergent political perspectives requiring careful alignment
- **Community Engagement**: Schools serve as community anchors requiring engagement with parents, taxpayers, business leaders, and civic organizations
- **Faculty Governance**: Universities have shared governance structures where faculty senate approval may be required for strategic initiatives
- **Student Voice**: Increasingly, students are recognized as key stakeholders in shaping school vision and priorities
- **Union Partnership**: Strategic alignment must include teacher and staff union leadership as implementation partners
- **Parent Advisory Groups**: Title I and other programs require formal parent advisory committees in planning processes
- **Alumni Networks**: Higher education must engage alumni as supporters, donors, and ambassadors for institutional vision
- **Regulatory Alignment**: Strategic vision must align with state education agency priorities and accreditation expectations
