## Industry Variations

Acquiring facilities and furnishings in education involves sector-specific considerations:

- **Learning Environment Design**: Facilities must support diverse pedagogical approaches including collaborative learning spaces, STEM labs, maker spaces, and flexible classrooms
- **Accessibility Requirements**: Schools must exceed ADA compliance to accommodate students with diverse learning needs and physical disabilities
- **Safety Standards**: Educational facilities require specialized security features, fire safety systems, and safe room designs
- **Technology Infrastructure**: Modern school buildings require robust network infrastructure, charging stations, and interactive display systems
- **Public Procurement Rules**: Public schools must follow competitive bidding requirements, often with local vendor preferences
- **Bond Financing**: Major facility acquisitions typically involve voter-approved bond measures with specific project scope requirements
- **Community Use Considerations**: School facilities often serve dual purposes for community events, requiring versatile design and scheduling systems
