## Industry Variations

Budget analysis in education involves distinct public sector requirements:

- **Fund Accounting**: Educational budgets use governmental fund accounting with separate funds for general operations, grants, capital, and debt service
- **State Funding Formulas**: Revenue analysis requires understanding complex weighted student funding calculations
- **Per-Pupil Spending Analysis**: Budgets are often analyzed on a per-student basis for comparison and equity purposes
- **Categorical Restrictions**: Many funding sources have specific use restrictions requiring careful budget allocation tracking
- **Multi-Year Projections**: Enrollment trends, salary schedules, and state funding changes require extended projection periods
- **Public Transparency**: Budget documents are public records with specific disclosure and hearing requirements
- **Site-Based Allocations**: Many districts allocate budgets to individual schools using weighted formulas
- **Reserve Requirements**: States often mandate minimum reserve levels that constrain budget flexibility
