## Industry Variations

IT portfolio alignment in educational institutions has specific characteristics:

- **Student Success Focus**: Technology investments must demonstrate connection to student achievement, engagement, and outcomes
- **Learning Management Centrality**: LMS platforms are often the core of the IT portfolio, requiring integration with other systems
- **Assessment Technology**: Portfolio must include tools for formative, summative, and diagnostic assessment aligned with curriculum
- **Equity Considerations**: IT investments must address digital divide issues including device access, connectivity, and digital literacy
- **Data System Integration**: Student information systems must connect with assessment, curriculum, and reporting platforms
- **FERPA-Compliant Architecture**: IT portfolio must ensure student data privacy across all applications and integrations
- **Accessibility Requirements**: All educational technology must meet accessibility standards for students with disabilities
- **Academic Calendar Timing**: Major IT implementations are typically scheduled during summer or break periods to minimize disruption
