## Industry Variations

Leave analysis in education involves unique patterns and considerations:

- **Substitute Cost Tracking**: Teacher absences directly trigger substitute costs that must be monitored and budgeted
- **Sabbatical Leave (Higher Ed)**: University faculty may take paid sabbaticals for research requiring long-term planning
- **Summer Leave Patterns**: Academic calendar creates different leave dynamics than year-round workplaces
- **Professional Development Leave**: Days allocated for training and conferences are tracked separately from personal leave
- **Sick Leave Banks**: Many districts have negotiated leave donation programs allowing employees to share accumulated sick days
- **FMLA Coordination**: Educational leave policies must coordinate with FMLA for eligible employees
- **Bereavement Policies**: Collective bargaining agreements often specify bereavement leave entitlements
- **Leave Buyback**: Some contracts allow employees to receive payment for unused leave at retirement
