# Review and report project performance

> Measuring the performance of a business project against key performance indicators including the project scope, schedule, quality, cost, and risk criteria.

## Overview

The review and report project performance process is a critical component of the Business Capabilities function within an organization. It encompasses the systematic approach to review and report project performance, ensuring that all activities are performed consistently, efficiently, and in alignment with organizational objectives. This process establishes the framework through which review and report project performance is executed, monitored, and continuously improved to deliver value across the enterprise.

Within the APQC Process Classification Framework (hierarchy 13.2.3.5), this activity supports the broader "Develop and Manage Business Capabilities" category. Effective execution requires cross-functional collaboration, clear accountability, and robust governance mechanisms. Organizations that mature this process typically see improved operational performance, reduced risk exposure, and stronger alignment between tactical activities and strategic goals.
